Phonetic Input Methods

Phonetic input methods input Hanzi by how they are pronounced, which is intuitive and maps well with how people usually think when writing. For example to input the sentence 早上好 ( zǎo shang hǎo ) — "Good morning" in Mandarin , the user inputs zao shang hao.

In Chinese

Mandarin Chinese

There are many IMEs available for Mandarin chinese as it is the official langauge of Mainland China. Since Phonetic IMEs depending on the orthography — how you "spell" — of pronunciation there has been a divide due to the two main methods to transcribe Mandarin Chinese, Pinyin and Zhuyin.

Pinyin

Pinyin ( 拼音 ( pīn yīn ) lit. "spelled sounds") is the most commonly used romanization system for Mandarin Chinese. It uses latin letters to transcribe sounds and diacritics to denote tones.

Zhuyin

Zhuyin ( 注音 ( zhù yīn ) lit. "phonetic notation") also commonly known as Bopomofo after the first four letters of the system: ㄅ, ㄆ, ㄇ and ㄈ, is the second most used system to transcribe Mandarin Chinese. Most popular in Taiwan, the system transcribes each of the possible sounds in Mandarin using 37 characters and 5 tone marks.

Other Chinese Languages

The main issue with Phonoetic IMEs is that they require transcription system to write down how letters are pronpunced. This causes other Chinese languages to be under-represented, as many of them do not have an "officially" recognized transcription system or said system are often relegated to 2nd citizenship. As such, for lower resource languages such as Sawndip or Ancient Chinese are best suited for Shape based or Stroke based input methods.
